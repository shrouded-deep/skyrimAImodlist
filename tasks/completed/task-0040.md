## Description

We have selected a per-city overhaul stack to replace JK's Skyrim AIO as the
city content layer. Before installing anything, research the compatibility of
each proposed mod against the existing Anvil list — specifically the
Spaghetti's suite and the city-touching mods already active.

**Key context:**
- We are NOT using JK's Skyrim AIO. These mods are chosen specifically because
  none require JK's.
- Spaghetti's is modular — individual per-city mods exist within the AIO and
  can be cherry-picked. If a proposed city mod conflicts with Spaghetti's
  coverage of the same city, the Spaghetti's component for that city can be
  dropped rather than the expansion mod. Note which Spaghetti's individual mod
  covers each city so we know what to drop.
- Focus on whether patches EXIST. Do not attempt to install or enable anything.

## Research methodology

For each proposed city mod, check its Nexus page (mod description,
requirements, and patch pages) for:

1. **Spaghetti's compatibility** — is there a named patch, or does the mod
   page document compatibility/incompatibility? If incompatible, note which
   Spaghetti's individual city module is the conflict (so it can be dropped).

2. **Whiterun-specific:** Does Capital Whiterun Expansion or Whiterun Has Walls
   Redone have patches for: Ivy Whiterun Well, Vanilla Plus City Entrances?
   Check Whiterun Has Walls Redone's patch hub specifically.

3. **Windhelm-specific:** Does Capital Windhelm Expansion have patches for:
   SB Fixed Windhelm Entrance, FYX Windhelm Graveyard, Ancient AF Windhelm?

4. **Riften-specific:** Does City of Crossed Daggers have patches for:
   FYX Riften Canal and Round Posts?

5. **Navigator — Navmesh Fixes:** This is the hardest one. Navigator makes
   broad NAVM edits across all cities. Check whether any of the proposed mods
   explicitly document Navigator compatibility or incompatibility.

6. **Lux Via:** Gate/road lighting. Check if any proposed mods document Lux Via
   patches or conflicts.
